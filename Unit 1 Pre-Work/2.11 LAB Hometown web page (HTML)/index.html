History

In 1701, Antoine de la Mothe Cadillac founded Fort Pontchartrain du Détroit, the future city of Detroit. The city's population became the 4th-largest in the nation in 1920, after only New York City, Chicago, and Philadelphia, with the expansion of the auto industry in the early 20th century.
